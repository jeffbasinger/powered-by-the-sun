Who's Powered By The Sun?

Since the 1990's, the world ramped up solar energy production.
... 30 years later, one country outperforms the rest of the world.

The world is moving slowly toward a renewable energy future, and solar is a major player. Today, the Australian continent is, per capita, the most prolific user of solar energy.

The techniology has come a long way since photovoltaic (PV) cells, invented nearly 150 years ago by American inventor Charles Fritts, were first put into use on a New York City rooftop in 1884.

While some homeowners use windmills or watermills, solar has become the most common individually purchased renewable energy source, now on over 25 million rooftops across the world, according to the International Energy Agency (IEA). Clean energy journal Solarquarter reports that Australia leads the world in per-capita rooftop PV installations as well.

PER CAPITA, AUSTRALIA PRODUCES THE MOST SOLAR

As a country, Australia had the most per capita solar production between commercial and residential solar sources in 2020. According to the Australian Government, they are targeting 82% renewable energy in their electrical grids by 2030.



Other countries that top the list of solar power per capita are Liechtenstein, Japan, Germany and the UAE. The United States comes in at number 18.

WORLD SOLAR PRODUCTION TIMELINE

The UN has been collecting data on gigawatt-hours of solar production around the world. In 1990, only 10 countries were producing significant energy using photovoltaic cells.

FROM 2000 TO 2020

By 2010, while Europe, Africa and the Middle East were making gains, Japan took the lead as the world's highest producer of solar power with 1,420 GWH produced that year. By contrast, the USA produced 1,124 GWH.

By 2020, China leads with most gigawatts from solar. But, their population size and energy requirements exceed other countries.

THE WORLD'S ENERGY SOURCES


Non-renewables, coal, oil, and natural gas are still the leading energy sources across the globe, at nearly 80%.

But seven countries -- Albania, Bhutan, Ethiopia, Iceland, Nepal, Paraguay, and the Democratic Republic of Congo -- now rely entirely on renewable energy, including geothermal, hydro, solar, and wind, according to the IEA.

Norway nearly joined the 100 percent club, sourcing 98.38% of its energy from renewables. Over 40 nations, including 11 in Europe, generate at least half their electricity from renewables, with countries like Germany and Portugal occasionally achieving 100% renewable energy production.


WHAT ABOUT WIND?

The world's leader in wind energy production is China, in 2020 creating over 385,000 GWH from wind turbines, even greater than their 260,000 GWH of solar production.
